Graduate School???

We encourage students to consider continuing their education after receiving their degree in Computer Science from Baylor University.  Many students have gone on to Master's and Doctoral degrees after receiving their Bachelor's from Baylor.   Below are some common questions concerning graduate school, along with possible sources for answers.

How do I find out about graduate programs?

If you decide to seek a master's degree in Computer Science, we encourage you to consider Baylor's graduate program in Computer Science.  Of course, there are many opportunities at other schools.  Simply find the universities you are interested in attending on the web.  Often they will provide instructions on what graduate programs they have and how to apply.

How can I afford graduate school?

Often students seeking a graduate degree in Computer Science can get funding from the university for performing tasks such as research and teaching assistance. The ACM publishes the Graduate Assistantship Directory which lists assistantship opportunities at some graduate programs. Fellowship programs have also been established to fund students seeking graduate degrees.  Some fellowships pay student tuition as well as a yearly salary.  Often the only requirement for receiving a fellowship is agreeing to go to graduate school so fellowships are a great opportunity. Baylor's Computer Science department actively encourages students to seek out fellowships.  We maintain a webpage listing advertised opportunities for fellowships.  Many graduate schools offer university fellowships to their own students so make sure you check with the specific universities in which you are interested for fellowship opportunities. (See examples from the University of Texas and Georgia Tech.)

What do I need to do to go to graduate school?

Application
Application deadlines can be as early as January so start working on your applications early.
Graduate Records Examination (GRE)
Most schools require the General GRE Test, and many Ph.D. programs also require the Computer Science Subject Test. Take these tests early in case you want to retake them. The general can be taken as a paper or computerized test; however, the subject test is only paper-based. Try the GRE web site for additional information.
Test of English as a Foreign Language (TOEFL)
Many schools require foreign students to take the TOEFL exam to demonstrate proficiency in the English language. Try the TOEFL web site for additional information.
Letters of Recommendation
Many programs require letters of recommendation. Start on this early to insure enough time for your recommendations to be written.
Purpose Statement
Many programs, especially doctoral, require the applicant to write a statement of purpose definining what they hope to accomplish in and after graduate school. This letter can be very important to the admission committee so take plenty of time in writing it and have it reviewed before submission.

May I "test drive" some graduate school courses during my time as an undergraduate?

Baylor computer science undergraduates wishing to "test drive" graduate school may take up to two graduate computer science courses in the last semester of their senior year. These courses cannot count towards an undergraduate degree; however, you may be able to transfer them to another graduate program. Please talk to Dr. Greg Speegle (email) about this option.
