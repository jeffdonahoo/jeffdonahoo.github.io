October Pre-K News

Dear Parents,

We are off to a great start in Pre-K!  We have been so busy learning and having fun that September seemed to fly by in an instant!  The children did a fantastic job of learning their shapes. They did some very creative projects using  squares, circles, rectangles, triangles, ovals, stars, and hearts.   They also had a great time singing songs, reading books, and doing art projects involving the colors red, yellow, blue, orange, green, purple, black, white, and pink.  They have been introduced to the lowercase letters "m," "a," and "t."  They know the sounds each makes and have been very proud to combine them to form and read several words!  They also have practiced counting up to ten objects and have been taught the numerals 1-10.  In September, we learned about our families and our five senses by doing a variety of cooking, art, reading, music, and center activities.  In order to help your child retain these concepts, please review them at home as often as possible.

October will be a wonderful month for our class!  We will be learning about bears in many fun ways. For example, we  will be reading in a bear cave, having a teddy bear picnic, and making coffee-sprinkled teddy bears.  We will also begin a unit on the beautiful changes that fall will bring to us!  We will be making stained glass window leaves, sorting fall vegetables, making pumpkin pie playdough, and participating many other interesting activities.  We will work with the letters "c," "s," "p," and "f," and continue to use their sounds to make new words.  On Monday, October 11th, we will have a student holiday.  On that day, you and your child are invited to attend a come- and-go reception in our room from 11:00 A.M. to noon to see what we have been doing at school.  On October 29th, the children may come to school dressed as their favorite  storybook characters.  We will have a special day of being creative with pumpkin projects, eating special snacks, playing fun games, and  sharing our favorite stories.

Here are some important dates to remember this month:

Oct. 6th- fall flower sale ends

Oct. 7th-  pick up pizza and cookie dough from 3:00-5:00 P.M.

Oct. 15th- last day of 1st 9 weeks

Oct.  16th- South Bosque Carnival 4:00-7:00 P.M.

Oct. 18th through 22nd- Red Ribbon Week

Oct. 29th- Storybook Character Dress Up Day

Please remember to check and empty your child's folder each day.  Talk to your child about what we are doing in school as you go through his/ her work.  Your interest and support will make a huge difference in your child's success this year. Fine motor control is a huge skill to achieve as a four or five year-old.  Please encourage your child to draw often, hold pencils and crayons correctly, and write letters and numbers as often as possible.  As always, feel free to call with any questions or concerns.  Our door is always open to you!

Sincerely,
